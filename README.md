## Portfolio Samples

The following samples are drawn from real support engineering work, debranded and restructured for portfolio presentation. All documents reflect the EchoCRS fictional platform — a hospitality CRS/PMS integration system created for this portfolio.

---

### 1. Troubleshooting Guide — Reservation Overbookings
`EchoCRS_Overbooking_TS_Guide.pdf`

A structured internal troubleshooting guide for investigating reservation overbooking incidents across transient and group booking types. Demonstrates:
- Branching investigation logic with clearly defined decision points
- Tiered escalation criteria (L1 → L2 → L3)
- Timestamp analysis workflow with timezone normalization requirements
- Escalation quick-reference decision matrix
- Audience: L1/L2 Support Engineers

---

### 2. Knowledge Base Article Collection — Connectivity & Integration
`EchoCRS_KB_Collection.pdf`

A collection of eight knowledge base articles covering the most common connectivity and integration failure scenarios in a SaaS hospitality platform. Demonstrates:
- Dual-audience writing within a single document: internal resolution steps (procedural, engineer-facing) alongside customer-facing communication blocks (clear, non-technical)
- KB article architecture with consistent metadata, keyword tagging, and category organization
- SSL/TLS, network connectivity, inventory, and code mapping scenarios
- Warning callouts distinguishing information engineers must not share with clients
- Audience: L1/L2 Support Engineers (internal); approved language blocks for client communication

---

### 3. Support Operations Impact Analysis — Integration Gateway Adoption
`EchoCRS_UseCase_Impact_Analysis.pdf`

A forward-looking operational analysis produced ahead of a major integration architecture change. Documents current-state support workflows across five scenarios, identifies tool dependencies at each support tier, and articulates the operational risk introduced if the new architecture does not maintain equivalent observability. Demonstrates:
- Systems-level thinking from a support engineering perspective
- Workflow documentation by role tier (L1, L2, L3, NOC, Client)
- Risk analysis tied to specific operational outcomes
- Future-state architectural requirements and open questions for engineering review
- Audience: Product, Engineering, and Support Leadership
